Security Journey

The Thailand Government Audit

How ethical hacking and responsible disclosure led to nationwide security improvements.

Discovery Phase

Early 2023

During authorized security research, I discovered multiple unpatched vulnerabilities across Thailand government web infrastructure, including critical SQL injection and XSS vulnerabilities.

SQL Injection XSS RCE

Penetration Testing

March 2023

Conducted controlled penetration testing to validate vulnerabilities and assess potential impact. Documented proof-of-concept exploits and data breach scenarios.

15+ Critical Vulnerabilities
3 Government Departments

Responsible Disclosure

April 2023

Reported findings through official channels with detailed technical reports, remediation recommendations, and offered assistance in fixing the vulnerabilities.

"The vulnerabilities were severe enough that citizen databases and sensitive government data were at immediate risk of exposure."

Collaborative Remediation

May - July 2023

Worked directly with government IT security teams to patch vulnerabilities, implement security measures, and establish continuous monitoring systems.

All critical vulnerabilities patched
New security protocols established
Ongoing security monitoring implemented

Recognition & Impact

August 2023

Acknowledged by Cyber Security Thailand for responsible disclosure. The incident led to improved national security policies and ongoing collaboration.

Official recognition from Cyber Security Thailand
Invited as security consultant for critical infrastructure

Key Takeaways

This experience reinforced important principles in cybersecurity:

  • No system is inherently secure - Constant vigilance and regular audits are essential
  • Ethical hackers are allies - White hat hackers help strengthen security, not undermine it
  • Responsible disclosure works - Working with organizations leads to better outcomes than public exposure
  • Security is a process - It requires continuous improvement and adaptation
